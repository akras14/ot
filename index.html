Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about our occupational therapy services

What is the difference between Occupational Therapy (OT) and Physical Therapy (PT)?

While Physical Therapy often focuses on improving gross motor skills, strength, and mobility, Occupational Therapy emphasizes helping individuals engage in meaningful daily activities. OT focuses on adapting tasks and environments to enhance a person's ability to live life to its fullest, addressing fine motor skills, cognitive function, and emotional well-being to achieve independence in everyday life.

Do you take insurance?

We are a private pay practice. We can provide you with a superbill that you can submit to your insurance company for potential out-of-network reimbursement. Please contact your insurance provider directly to confirm your benefits.

How long is each session?

Each session typically lasts for approximately one hour. This duration allows for a comprehensive and personalized approach to your therapy needs, including assessment, treatment, and goal-setting.

What are your service areas?

We primarily serve Santa Clarita & Santa Clarita Valley, Burbank, Pasadena, Beverly Hills, Calabasas, Glendale, Westwood, Thousand Oaks, Santa Monica, North Hollywood, and Marina Del Ray, Malibu. Further locations are a possibility and can be arranged upon discussion.

What should I expect during the first appointment?

Your first appointment will include a comprehensive head-to-toe functional assessment, covering range of motion, sensation, vision, balance, a home assessment, and an evaluation of equipment needs. We will also review your medical history to develop a personalized treatment plan with clear goals.

Do you offer tele-health services?

Yes, we offer flexible tele-health and hybrid therapy options to accommodate your needs, ensuring you receive quality care from the comfort of your home.
